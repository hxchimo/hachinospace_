The Playlist Reflection Activity


‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Last week, January 5, 2024, we were assigned to view “The Playlist” on Netflix.
When I was informed of this, I thought, “How can watching a series have any relevant
connection to our subject?” But later on, I proved myself wrong. After watching the first
episode, I remembered something crucial I forgot when I asked myself the question.
I forgot how a playliststarts with a code, and a code starts with an idea.

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Episode 1 introduced me to Daniel Ek, the CEO of Spotify. In the episode, I
learned that he dropped out of college and worked for different companies. He also
applied for a job at Google but was soon rejected because he didn't have a college
degree. Later on, he founded Advertigo, an online marketing firm that he sold to
Tradedoubler. At this point, I pondered about the typical belief of success—
that to become successful, one must undergo education and complete college.
But this was proven wrong by Daniel Ek when he made $1.25 million at the age
of 23. Upon knowing this, I was shocked and amazed by his talents, and skills in
web development and programming. Especially because during that digital age,
resources are limited and technologies are not as developed as it is, presently.

‎ ‎ ‎ When the peer-to-peer music service, Napster, shut down, he thought of
having an ad-free music service where people could listen to music anywhere,
anytime, without paying and downloading to listen. And so, he realized that
music piracy is a problem and it can only be solved by providing a better music
server, benefitting its listeners and artists. Thus, driven by this thought, he started
the idea of Spotify. He and his company cautiously and carefully planned its
development over the years. I specifically like the music choices in the episode.
I like how they incorporate music according to the scenario because it means a lot
in setting the mood and shows how Spotify is heavily influenced by music.

‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ While I was watching, I could not take my attention off the screen. I was
impressed and captivated throughout the episode as a Spotify user. Although I
use the app almost daily, I have never thought of how it came to be. And now
that I knew, I instantly felt interested and began to appreciate Spotify more.
From codes as its foundation to creating the play button to having it play smoothly
without delay, Spotify became what it is today. It is a well-known music app used
worldwide with over 500 million users.

Overall, the first episode is fascinating and quite entertaining to watch.
The way it ends makes us, the watchers, excited to know what happens next. I
liked it so much that when we exited the computer lab, I immediately thought
that it was unlike any other. I realized how codes can be made into something
as great as Spotify with proper determination, knowledge, and skills, for it
to work and become functional.
